The Causes of Climate Change

Understanding the human activities and natural processes driving global warming

Primary Drivers of Climate Change

They are human activities that contribute to climate change but these human activities are the main contributors to the greenhouse effect and global warming

Burning Fossil Fuels

Coal, oil, and gas combustion for electricity, heat, and transportation accounts for over 75% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

Learn more

Deforestation

Cutting down forests removes carbon sinks and releases stored CO2, responsible for about 10% of global warming.

Learn more

Agriculture

Livestock, fertilizers, and rice cultivation produce methane and nitrous oxide - potent greenhouse gases.

Learn more

Urbanization

Expanding cities increase energy demand, create heat islands, and reduce natural carbon absorption areas.

Learn more

Consumerism

Manufacturing, transporting, and disposing of goods creates emissions at every stage of the product lifecycle.

Learn more

Transportation

Cars, trucks, ships, and planes burning fossil fuels account for nearly 20% of global CO2 emissions.

Learn more
