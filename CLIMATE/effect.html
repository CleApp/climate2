Climate Change Timeline

Key events and projections showing the progression of climate change impacts

1950s

Early Warnings

Scientists begin documenting rising CO2 levels and potential warming effects, but impacts are not yet widely visible.

1980s

First Observable Effects

Global temperature rise becomes statistically significant. Ozone hole discovery raises environmental awareness.

2000s

Accelerating Changes

Arctic sea ice declines rapidly. Extreme weather events increase in frequency. IPCC reports gain global attention.

2020s

Widespread Impacts

Record-breaking temperatures, wildfires, and storms occur globally. Climate refugees emerge as sea levels rise.

2050 Projections

Critical Thresholds

Without significant action, we may pass irreversible tipping points like Amazon dieback and permafrost thaw.
